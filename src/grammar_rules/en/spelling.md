# Spelling & Grammar Rules — Radiology Reports (EN)

## Sentence Structure
- Every sentence must begin with a capital letter and end with a period.
- Use the active voice where possible: "The scan shows…" not "It is shown by the scan that…"
- Avoid sentence fragments; each clause must have a subject and verb.

## Common Radiology Dictation Errors
- "there is" / "there are" — check number agreement with the subject
- "which" vs "that" — use "that" for restrictive clauses, "which" (with comma) for non-restrictive
- "normal" is an adjective, not a verb: "The lung fields are normal" ✓ — "The lung fields normal" ✗
- Do not start a sentence with a numeral; write it out: "Three lesions…" not "3 lesions…" at sentence start

## Numbers and Measurements
- Write measurements in numerals: 3 cm, 5 mm, 2.5 cm × 1.8 cm
- Use × (multiplication sign) between dimensions, not "x" or "*"
- Separate value and unit with a space: "4 cm" not "4cm"
- Ranges: "5–10 mm" (en dash, no spaces) — not "5-10 mm" or "5 to 10 mm"

## Punctuation
- Use a comma after introductory clauses: "On the current examination, there is…"
- Colons introduce lists or elaborations: "Three findings are noted: …"
- Semicolons separate independent but related clauses

## Agreement
- Collective nouns are singular: "The series of images shows…"
- "data" is plural: "The data suggest…"
- Anatomy agreement: "The right and left lungs are…" (plural verb)

## Do Not Modify
- Patient names, doctor names, hospital names
- Dates and numerical values
- Existing paragraph breaks and structural formatting
